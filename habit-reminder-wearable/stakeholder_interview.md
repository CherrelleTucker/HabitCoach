# Stakeholder Interview Guide
## Physical Therapist / Entrepreneur Perspective

**Interview Date:** _______________
**Interviewee Name:** _______________
**Role/Practice:** _______________
**Interview Duration:** ~30-45 minutes

---

## Purpose

Validate the concept of a haptic reminder app for breaking bad movement habits during physical activity. Gather insights on PT workflows, patient needs, and potential B2B opportunities.

---

## Section 1: Background & Context

### About Their Practice

1. **Tell me about your practice.** What types of patients/conditions do you primarily work with?

2. **What percentage of your patients** are recovering from injuries where they've developed compensatory movement patterns?

3. **How do patients typically do with home exercise programs (HEPs)?** What's the biggest challenge?

---

## Section 2: The Problem Space

### Compensation Patterns & Habit Breaking

4. **When patients develop bad movement habits during injury recovery, how do you currently address that?**
   - In-session corrections?
   - Verbal cues they're supposed to remember?
   - Any tools or apps you recommend?

5. **What happens between sessions?** How do patients remember to self-correct when you're not there?

6. **Can you describe a specific patient case** where breaking a compensation pattern was particularly challenging? What made it difficult?

7. **How long does it typically take** to break a movement compensation habit? Days? Weeks? Months?

### Current Solutions

8. **Do you currently recommend any apps or devices** to help patients with reminders, posture, or habit correction?
   - If yes: What do you like/dislike about them?
   - If no: Why not? What's missing from the market?

9. **Have you heard of or used any of these products?**
   - UPRIGHT GO (posture device)
   - Pulse Mindfulness Ring
   - Any interval timer apps

---

## Section 3: Concept Validation

### Present the Concept

*Read or paraphrase:*

> "We're exploring an app that delivers haptic (vibration) reminders at set intervals during physical activity. The idea is: a patient starts a session, puts their phone away or wears a smartwatch, and gets buzzed every X minutes as a cue to check their form, posture, or break a specific habit. It would have saved session profiles, voice activation, and varying intensity to prevent desensitization."

### Reaction & Feedback

10. **What's your initial reaction to this concept?**

11. **Does this solve a real problem you see in your practice?** On a scale of 1-10, how valuable would this be?

12. **What use cases come to mind immediately?** Which patient types or conditions would benefit most?

13. **What concerns or objections come to mind?**

14. **Would this work better on:**
    - Phone only (in pocket)
    - Smartwatch (Apple Watch, etc.)
    - Dedicated wearable device
    - Why?

---

## Section 4: Feature Priorities

### Must-Have vs Nice-to-Have

15. **Rank these features by importance (1 = most important):**

| Feature | Rank (1-7) | Notes |
|---------|------------|-------|
| Customizable interval timing | | |
| Saved session profiles | | |
| Voice activation (start/stop) | | |
| Varying vibration intensity | | |
| Therapist-created presets | | |
| Progress tracking/history | | |
| Verbal/audio cues option | | |

16. **What features are missing from this list** that would make you more likely to recommend it?

17. **Would you want the ability to create custom presets** that you could share with patients? (e.g., "Dr. Smith's Knee Rehab Protocol")

---

## Section 5: Business Model & Adoption

### B2B Potential

18. **Would you personally recommend this app to patients?** Why or why not?

19. **What would make you MORE likely to recommend it?**
    - Clinical validation/studies?
    - Endorsement by PT associations?
    - Integration with existing PT software?
    - White-label/branded version for your practice?

20. **Would you pay for a "pro" or "clinic" version** that let you manage patient presets? What would that be worth?

### Patient Adoption

21. **What percentage of your patients have smartphones?** Smartwatches?

22. **What barriers would prevent patients from using an app like this?**
    - Technical literacy?
    - Cost?
    - Remembering to use it?
    - Other?

23. **What price point would be reasonable for patients?**
    - Free with ads?
    - $2-5 one-time?
    - $5-10 one-time?
    - $2-5/month subscription?

---

## Section 6: Broader Market

### Other Use Cases

24. **Beyond PT, where else do you see this being useful?**
    - Sports training?
    - Music practice?
    - Anxiety/grounding?
    - Other?

25. **Do you know other professionals** (coaches, trainers, other therapists) who might find this valuable?

### Competition & Differentiation

26. **What would make this app stand out** from generic timer apps or existing solutions?

27. **Is there anything similar you've seen** that we should be aware of?

---

## Section 7: Wrap-Up

28. **If this app existed today, would you try it yourself?** Would you recommend it to patients this week?

29. **What's the ONE thing** that would make this a must-have vs nice-to-have?

30. **Is there anything else** you think we should know or consider?

31. **Would you be open to:**
    - Testing an early prototype?
    - Connecting us with patients who might benefit?
    - Providing feedback on future iterations?
